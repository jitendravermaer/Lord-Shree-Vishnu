दिव्य लोक (Divine Realms of Svarg Lok)

Amaravati

अमरावती
Capital City of Swarga Loka
Divine Description:
The magnificent capital city of Indra, built entirely of gold and precious gems. Contains the divine assembly hall Sudharma where all gods gather for cosmic decisions, the celestial garden Nandana with wish-fulfilling trees, and palaces that defy earthly architecture.
Sacred Features:
Sudharma Hall Nandana Garden Mandakini River Crystal Palaces Divine Assembly Celestial Courts

Nandana Vana

नन्दन वन
Celestial Garden Paradise
Divine Description:
The divine garden of eternal spring where celestial flowers bloom perpetually and divine fragrances fill the air. Home to the Kalpavriksha (wish-fulfilling tree), Parijata tree with heavenly fragrance, and countless other magical plants.
Sacred Features:
Kalpavriksha Parijata Tree Apsara Dances Divine Music Eternal Spring Celestial Flowers

Kshira Sagara

क्षीर सागर
Cosmic Ocean of Milk
Divine Description:
The primordial ocean of milk where Lord Vishnu rests on the serpent Shesha. This cosmic ocean was churned by gods and demons to obtain the nectar of immortality. Its waters have healing properties and contain the essence of all creation.
Sacred Features:
Shesha Serpent Amrita Source Seven Islands Cosmic Wisdom Healing Waters Divine Rest

Mount Meru

मेरु पर्वत
Cosmic Axis Mountain
Divine Description:
The golden mountain at the center of the universe, serving as the cosmic axis around which all celestial bodies revolve. Its peak reaches into the highest heavens while its base extends deep into the earth.
Sacred Features:
Golden Peak Four Gem Faces Cosmic Axis Celestial Gateway Universal Center Divine Residence

देव पदानुक्रम (Divine Hierarchies)

Trimurti

त्रिमूर्ति
Supreme Trinity
Divine Members:
Brahma (Creator), Vishnu (Preserver), Shiva (Destroyer/Transformer) - The three primary aspects of the Supreme Divine governing creation, preservation, and transformation of the universe.

Indra

इन्द्र
King of Gods
Divine Role:
Ruler of Swarga, Commander of Divine Forces, Leader of all Devas in cosmic governance and protection of dharma throughout the universe.

Dikpala

दिक्पाल
Directional Guardians
Divine Role:
Eight Directional Guardians protecting cosmic boundaries - Indra (East), Agni (Southeast), Yama (South), Nirrti (Southwest), Varuna (West), Vayu (Northwest), Kubera (North), Ishana (Northeast).

Navagraha

नवग्रह
Nine Planetary Deities
Divine Role:
Nine Planetary Deities governing cosmic influences and human destiny through celestial movements and astrological effects on all living beings.

देव त्योहार (Divine Festivals)

Indra Puja

इन्द्र पूजा
Ashwin Shukla Chaturdashi
Festival Description:
A grand celebration honoring Indra, the king of gods, for his protection and blessings of rain and prosperity. Devotees perform elaborate rituals, offer prayers for good monsoons, and celebrate with dance and music.
Sacred Significance:
Ensures good rainfall, agricultural prosperity, protection from natural disasters, and divine blessings for leadership and courage.

Chandra Darshan

चन्द्र दर्शन
Every New Moon & Full Moon
Festival Description:
Monthly celebrations dedicated to Chandra, the moon god, observed on new moon and full moon days. Devotees fast, meditate, and offer prayers to gain mental peace, emotional stability, and spiritual insight.
Sacred Significance:
Brings mental clarity, emotional balance, fertility blessings, and enhanced intuitive abilities through lunar energy.

Surya Jayanti

सूर्य जयन्ती
Magha Shukla Saptami
Festival Description:
The birthday celebration of Surya, the sun god, marked by early morning prayers, sun salutations, and offerings of water mixed with red flowers. Devotees perform Surya Namaskar and recite Aditya Hridayam.
Sacred Significance:
Grants vitality, health, success, leadership qualities, and removes darkness from life through solar blessings.

Agni Purnima

अग्नि पूर्णिमा
Kartik Purnima
Festival Description:
A sacred fire festival dedicated to Agni, the fire god, featuring elaborate fire ceremonies, homas, and yajnas. Devotees light sacred fires, offer ghee and grains, and perform purification rituals.
Sacred Significance:
Purifies negative karma, enhances digestive fire, brings divine blessings, and establishes connection with all deities through Agni.
